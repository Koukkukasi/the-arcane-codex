THE ARCANE CODEX

The Core Innovation: Asymmetric Information

In The Arcane Codex, you and your companions enter the cursed kingdom of Valdria. The gods have abandoned this realm, but they still watch and judge your every action.

The revolutionary mechanic: Each player sees different information about the same situation.

Example Scenario

What Everyone Sees:
"You enter an abandoned warehouse at midnight. Grimsby's daughter cries from behind a locked door. The merchant who hired you watches nervously. 'Please,' he begs, 'save her quickly!'"
FIGHTER'S WHISPER
"Your combat instincts scream danger. This is an ambush. You count at least six hidden figures in the shadows."
MAGE'S WHISPER
"You sense powerful illusion magic. That's not a child crying - it's a magical recording. This is a trap."
THIEF'S WHISPER
"You recognize Grimsby's tell - he's lying. His 'daughter' doesn't exist. He's working with bandits."
CLERIC'S WHISPER
"Your divine sense detects no innocent soul beyond that door. Only darkness and malice wait within."

The Dilemma

Now the real game begins:

  • Do you share your whisper with the party?
  • Do you trust what others claim to know?
  • What if someone is lying about their whisper?
  • What if acting on incomplete information gets someone killed?

Every choice affects:

Party Trust: 50/100

  • Party Trust: Share information honestly = +10. Lie or hide info = -10.
  • NPC Reactions: Grimsby might turn hostile if exposed.
  • Divine Judgment: The gods judge if you let innocents suffer or betray allies.

How It Works

1-4 Players: Each player gets their own unique whisper based on their class.

Real-time Discussion: Players must quickly decide what to share before time runs out.

Consequences: Your choices ripple through the entire adventure.

The game tracks:

  • Who shares information (builds trust)
  • Who keeps secrets (maintains advantage but loses trust)
  • Who lies (might win short-term but destroys relationships)

The Stakes

When trust falls below 30:

  • NPCs with IMPULSIVE flaws act without thinking
  • GREEDY companions steal from the party
  • COWARDLY allies abandon you in danger
  • The gods themselves may intervene... harshly

This isn't just about winning battles. It's about navigating the complex web of trust, deception, and survival in a world where information is power and no one sees the complete picture.
